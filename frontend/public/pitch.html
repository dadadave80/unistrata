01 — The problem

LPs are forced sellers of volatility — with no buyer.

Every AMM liquidity provider is implicitly short gamma. When price moves, arbitrage picks them off — impermanent loss, or LVR. There's no way to opt out of that risk and simply earn yield.

It is the largest unhedged short-volatility position in DeFi — and nobody is on the other side of the trade.

02 — The insight

That volatility has a price.

The value bled to arbitrage per unit of time is the cost of being short the pool's variance. It is a real, measurable cashflow — and no one was selling it back to LPs as a product.

LVR ≈ σ² / 8  per unit time — the price of the pool's variance

So we split one LP position into two: a buyer and a seller of that variance.

03 — The product

Two strata.
One pool.

Sediment junior · first-loss

Absorbs impermanent loss first. In return, keeps the swap fees and the coupon premium.

→ the volatility seller

Bedrock senior · protected

Principal held flat. Earns a fixed coupon, priced from the pool's own realized variance.

→ the volatility buyer
04 — How it works · the oracle

The pool prices its own risk.

In afterSwap, the hook squares each block's tick move into an on-chain realized-variance accumulator. The Bedrock coupon is set straight from it — calm pool, cheap coupon; violent pool, Sediment gets paid more.

No external oracle. No price feed to manipulate. The pool is the oracle.

05 — How it settles

Settles itself. Across chains.

A Reactive Network contract on Lasna subscribes to the hook's variance stream on Unichain. When variance spikes past the trigger, it fires a cross-chain callback that settles the tranches — instantly, autonomously.
